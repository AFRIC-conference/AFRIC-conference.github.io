Datum und Location

28. Oktober 2023 Herthadalen, Dänemark

Wir werden die Trauung, Hochzeitsempfang und Dinner/Party im Restaurant Herthadalen feiern, seit 1788 ein Ort für Feste und Feiern. Das Restaurant liegt am Ende der längsten Allee Nordeuropas und mitten in Sjællands erstem Nationalpark, Skjoldungernes Land. Es gibt viel Wald und Natur zu entdecken, also wenn man sich die Beine vertreten will, ist hier der Platz dafür. Man kann zum Beispiel Hertha’s Altar besichtigen - einst ein Platz, an dem der Fruchtbarkeitsgöttin geopfert wurde, dann wurde es zum Rednerpult, an dem N.F.S. Grundtvig seine erste Verfassungsreden gehalten haben soll.

Praktisches

Transport

Das Restaurant ist mitten im Nichts, weswegen es schwierig ist, es mit öffentlichen Verkehrsmitteln zu erreichen. Von der nähsten Bushaltestelle ist es immer noch ein 30-minütiger Spaziergang zum Restaurant. Wir werden deswegen Bustransport zum und vom Restaurant arrangieren. Hierfür ist es wichtig, dass Ihr möglichstbald den Anmeldungsfragebogen ausfüllt!
Generell ist es am einfachsten, mit dem Zug oder dem Flugzeug nach Kopenhagen zu reisen und dann von da aus nach Roskilde weiterzufahren.
Sowohl der Copenhagen Airport als auch der Hauptbahnhof sind gut an das Zug-, Metro- und Busnetzwerk angeschlossen. Internationale Züge von schwedischen Städten und Hamburg fahren den Hauptbahnhof mehrmals am Tag an. Roskilde (die näheste grössere Stadt für Hotels) ist innerhalb von 25 Minuten vom Kopenhagener Hauptbahnhof erreichbar. Google Maps oder Rejseplanen sind gute Apps/Webseiten, um Transport zu planen, vor allem letztere hat die neuesten Information überZugausfälle.

Accommodation

Herthadalen is approximately 30 minutes away from central Copenhagen (by car), but the closest bigger city is Roskilde. We can get a deal with the Comwell Roskilde hotel, that is working together with Restaurant Herthadalen. A small standard twin bed room is DKK 975 (approx. EUR 130.50) incl. breakfast (13 m2, twin beds directly next to each other). Upgrades are available. To book, fill in our RSVP ASAP. You can obviously also use booking.com or other websites to find accommodation. A note on AirBnB: in Denmark, you as a guest are responsible for ensuring that the owner pays taxes on earnings. Please ask for a receipt/proof if you use AirBNB!

Children

The location is child-friendly, with a big swing and football field in the garden- and a forest just outdoor! Children are welcome everywhere and at every time during the wedding! Nevertheless, we have reserved Orangeriet, a room at the venue as a place for children to play and sleep later in the night. So parents, no excuse not to dance! We plan to lay down mattrasses on the floor and bring blankets later at night. It would be great if parents could bring favourite toys, and noise-cancelling headphones might help, too. If you bring a child under 2, we will contact you regarding high chairs- the restaurant has some available, but we will check whether they have enough.

Things To Do in the Area

København & Surroundings

Denmark’s capital is a must-see and its centre only 30 minutes away from the wedding location/Roskilde. There’s plenty of things to do and see: castles, towers, beaches, squares etc. A good list to start is here

Things Tested by Ourselves:
  • Drink a cheap beer at Fingerbøllet in our street, or test Copenhagen's beer bars with larger selections (incl. German beer; fx, Bootlegger's close to Torvehallerne or on Island's Brygge, the Taphouse, or the cozy Godt Øl)
  • Two Socks Gin and Tonic bar for the G&T lovers among us
  • Enjoy a hyggelig game or two (or three...) at Bastard Café (table reservations need to be made the day before, but you should find a space when you are trying outside of weekend evening hours)
  • Eat at Sankt Annæ 8 (reservations recommended, closed on Sundays; new day- new menu)
  • Test burgers at Fatty's (our favourite as you can combine with a beer at Bootlegger's) or Gasoline Grill
  • Vor Frelsers Kirke (for free) and its tower (costs)
  • Christiania- off of Pusher Street, it is beautiful and chill!
  • The world's second-oldest amusement park Tivoli will be decorated in the Halloween spirit, so brace yourself for pumpkin competitions and horror houses.
Our recommendations for spending time outside of Copenhagen are:
  • Jægersborg Dyrehave: a nice way to spend half a day in half-nature. The deer park is an enclosed area, so deer are running around freely. You can have a stroll or a picknick in open fields and forests. We’ve never been here without seeing deer close by, and it’s a nice train ride along the coast (20 minutes from København H). Bakken is also close, the oldest amusement park in the world (unlike Tivoli in Copenhagen, entrance is free, so you only pay for attractions; it’s less beautiful though).
  • Helsingør and Kronborg Slot: A small city now, Helsingør (or Elsinore in English) used to be an important trade city. It’s situated where the Øresund is narrowest, so Denmark taxed all ships sailing towards and from the Baltic Sea. Kronborg Slot is famous for having inspired Shakespeare’s Hamlet. You can also find the Danish Maritime Museum and Han (he), a steel statue dubbed by some as the little brother of The Little Mermaid in Copenhagen. When you do the trip, eat at the food market Værftets Madmarked
  • Louisiana: a world-renowned museum for modern and contemporary art, Louisiana also has a very beautiful park to have a picknick in (weather permitting). From København H, it takes ca. 50 minutes by train and bus.

Roskilde

Situated in an important area for Vikings and founded by Harald Bluetooth in the 10th century, Roskilde was once the capital of Denmark. It’s now famous for three things:
  • The Roskilde Festival: one of Europe’s largest festivals will not be on during the wedding weekend, but there’s Ragnarock, an interactive museum about modern music
  • Vikingeskibsmuseet: A museum around all things viking, including five original viking boats sunk intentionally by the Vikings in the 11th century and recovered in the 1960s
  • Roskilde Domkirke: UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1996, this cathedral is also home of 39 tombs of Danish kings and queens- including for the current Queen Margrethe II of Denmark
Obviously, the national park of the wedding location is also something to explore: Skjoldungernes Land.
