Date and Location

October 28th, 2023 Herthadalen, Denmark

We’ll have the wedding ceremony and dinner/party at Restaurant Herthadalen, a location for celebrations since 1788. It lies at the end of Northern Europe’s longest alley and inside Sjællands first national park, Skjoldungernes Land. There is plenty of forest and nature to explore, so if you fancy an autumnal walk before the ceremony and reception, this is the place to go! You can for example visit Hertha’s altar/lectern - once a place for worshipping the fertility goddess of the same name, then transformed into a lectern where N.F.S. Grundtvig is said to have held his first constitutional speeches.

Practicalities

Transportation

The restaurant is in the middle of nowhere, so it is difficult to get there by public transport. The closest bus station is still a 30 minute walk away from the location. We will arrange a bus to get you all to and from Restaurant Herthadalen. For this, it is important you fill in our RSVP survey!
Generally, it’s easiest to either take the train to Copenhagen or fly. Both Copenhagen Airport and the central station are well connected to the train, metro, and bus networks, and international trains from Sweden or Hamburg arrive and depart several times daily. Roskilde (the nearest bigger city and closest spot for accommodation) is reachable within 25 minutes from the Copenhagen central station. Google Maps or Rejseplanen are good Apps/Websites to use to get around, the latter offers the most recent information on strikes/train cancellations.

Accommodation

Herthadalen is approximately 30 minutes away from central Copenhagen (by car), but the closest bigger city is Roskilde. We can get a deal with the Comwell Roskilde hotel, that is working together with Restaurant Herthadalen. A small standard twin bed room is DKK incl. breakfast (13 m2, twin beds directly next to each other). Upgrades are available. To book, fill in our RSVP ASAP. A note on AirBnB: in Denmark, you as a guest are responsible for ensuring that the owner pays taxes. Please check if you use it!

Children

The location is child-friendly, with a big swing and football field in the garden- and a forest just outdoor! Children are welcome everywhere and at every time! Nevertheless, we have reserved Orangeriet, a room at the venue as a place for children to play and sleep later in the night. So parents, no excuse not to dance! We plan to lay down mattrasses on the floor and to bring blankets. It would be great if parents could bring favourite toys, potentially noise-cancelling headphones might help, too.

Things To Do in the Area

København & Surroundings

Denmark’s capital is a must-see and its centre only 30 minutes away from the wedding location/Roskilde. There’s plenty of things to do and see: castles, towers, beaches, squares etc. A good list to start is here.
Things Tested by Ourselves: • Drink a cheap beer at Fingerbøllet • Eat at Sankt Annæ 8 (reservations recommended, closed on Sundays; new day- new menu; meat dish viewable online, vegetarian online on-site) • Two Socks Gin and Tonic bar • Vor Frelsers Kirke (for free) and its tower (costs) • Christiania- off of Pusher Street, beautiful and chill! Our recommendations for spending time outside of Copenhagen are: • Jaegersborg Dyrehave: a nice way to spend half a day in half-nature. The deer park is an enclosed area, so deer are running around freely. You can have a stroll or a picknick in open field and forests. We’ve never been here without seeing deer close by, and it’s a nice train ride along the coast (20 minutes from København H). Bakken is also close, the oldest amusement park in the world (unlike Tivoli in Copenhagen, entrance is free, so you pay for attractions you visit only; it’s less beautiful though). • Helsingør and Kronborg Slot: A small city now, Helsingør (or Elsinore in English) used to be an important trade city. It’s situated where the Øresund is narrowest, so Denmark taxed all ships sailing towards and from the Baltic Sea. Kronborg Slot is famous for having inspired Shakespeare’s Hamlet. You can also find the Danish Maritime Museum and Han (he), a steel statue dubbed by some as the little brother of The Little Mermaid in Copenhagen. • Louisiana: a world-renowned museum for modern and contemporary art, Louisiana also has a very beautiful park to have a picknick in (weather permitting). From København H, it takes ca. 50 minutes by train and bus.

Roskilde

Situated in an important area for Vikings and founded by Harald Bluetooth in the 10th century, Roskilde was once the capital of Denmark. It’s now famous for three things: • The Roskilde Festival: one of Europe’s largest festivals will not be on during the wedding weekend, but there’s Ragnarock, an interactive museum about modern music. • Vikingeskibsmuseet: A museum around all things viking, including five original viking boats sunk intentionally by the Vikings in the 11th century and recovered in the 1960s • Roskilde Domkirke: UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1996, this cathedral is also home of 39 tombs of Danish kings and queens- including for the current Queen Margrethe II of Denmark Obviously, the national park of the wedding location is also something to explore: Skjoldungernes Land
